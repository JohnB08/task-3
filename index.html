I am an avid reader, allthough audiobooks are my primary modus operandus these days.
In the wast collection of fiction, as a reader you feel overwhelmed and defeated by the sheer amount of stories available.
So, as a philosophy I read as many one books by a single author as possible,
and selected a few that, as a goal, I would consume everything.

These are my current favourite authors:

The Weird.

Haruki Murakami is a very dificult author to like. His books are often so bizzare it borders on the absurd.
Yet, when he shines he truly shines.
It is said when you read your first Murakami book; you either fall in love, or you never read him again.
The first book of his I read was "Kafka on the shore", a beautiful book of lacking identity when growing up.
I cannot recommend it enough, if you can handle the abstract nature the story thrives in.

The Bad One

Whenever you browse literary forums, they'll allways say the same thing:
Stephen King is a bad writer.
You can say he might lack the big lofty philosophical goals of modern literature,
yet he has earned his nomenclature: "The greatest storyteller alive" by simply writing excellent stories.
Stories about horrible horrible stuff, yet captivating and engaging. Oldschool fairytales for a modern audience.

The Author Who Hated Writing

Douglas Adams created what might be the single best collection of modern satire ever written with his "Hitchhiker" books.
Yet he hated writing them, often being hounded by his publisist and his agent to complete a new book. Which he did, eventually, and in protest.
"The Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy" Is still one of the greatest comedic works ever made, and the rest of the books are almost as good.

The Satirist That Died Writing

The story of Mikhail Bulgakov is a sad one. Forced into medicine by his parents, later found solace in writing.
A prolific writer of satire in the 20's,
yet in many ways it was writing that ended his life. In 1928 he started what would be his best, and last work.
"The Master and Margarita" was supposed to be his ultimate satire on life in the Soviet Union.
Yet, just as the author-character in the book, Mikhail was never satisfied.
He died, book unfinished in 1940.
In the end his wife completed the book, and it was released in the 60s for everyone to read.
The book apparently stressed him out, to the point of absolute exhaustion, still it's a masterpiece in fiction.

The Timetravelers

I lied when I said Animals as Leaders were the most out there band. Because nothing tops this.
A group composed of historical culture and language nerds, their goal is to find old germanic texts and transpose them to song.
There is something brutal and pure about their songs, a weird beauty that can't easily be described,
other than it hits a more primal part of me.
It engages me on a different level than most all other music.
