These are my current favourite authors:

The Weird.

Haruki Murakami is a very dificult author to like. His books are often so bizzare it borders on the absurd.
Yet, when he shines he truly shines.
It is said when you read your first Murakami book; you either fall in love, or you never read him again.
The first book of his I read was "Kafka on the shore", a beautiful book of lacking identity when growing up.
I cannot recommend it enough, if you can handle the abstract nature the story thrives in.

The Bad One

Whenever you browse literary forums, they'll allways say the same thing:
Stephen King is a bad writer.
You can say he might lack the big lofty philosophical goals of modern literature,
yet he has earned his nomenclature: "The greatest storyteller alive" by simply writing excellent stories.
Stories about horrible horrible stuff, yet captivating and engaging. Oldschool fairytales for a modern audience.
